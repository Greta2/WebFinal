Precautions

The Pulmonary Fibrosis Foundation is closely monitoring the impact of the coronavirus outbreak. People with chronic illnesses, such as pulmonary fibrosis, may be at higher risk for developing complications from COVID-19. There are several everyday precautions that individuals can take to help avoid infection from the virus. These include staying home as much as possible, limiting contact with those outside your household and avoiding non-essential visits. As telehealth options are expanding, PF patients should contact their doctors about routine visits and testing.

Don't stress out. Have fun and play with us. Pay attention to the following advices :)

Call your health care provider or COVID-19 hotline to find out where and when to get a test. If testing is not available, stay home and away from others for 14 days. Monitor yourself for any symptoms for 14 days.

While you are in quarantine, do not go to work, to school or to public places. Ask someone to bring you supplies. Keep the room well-ventilated.

Keep at least a 1-metre distance from others, even from your family members. If you share a room, place beds at least 1 metre apart.

Wear a medical mask to protect others, including if/when you need to seek medical care.

Clean your hands frequently.

Stay positive by keeping in touch with loved ones by phone or online, and by exercising at home.
